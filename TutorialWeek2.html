Q2.Where is epithelial tissue found in the body?Describe differences in function between simpleepithelia and stratified epithelia

  • Simple epithelia
    • Contain one layer of cells (so all cells touch thebasement membrane)
  • Stratified epithelia
    • Contain more than one layer (some cells sit on top ofothers and do not touch the basement membrane)

Q2.Where is epithelial tissue found in the body?Describe differences in function between simpleepithelia and stratified epithelia

  • Simple epithelia
    • Permits rapid diffusion or transport of substances
      • Secretes serous fluid, mucus
  • Stratified epithelia
    • Resist abrasion
    • Retards water loss through the skin
    • Resist penetration by pathogens
    • Secretes sweat
    • Production of sperm and ovarian hormones

  • Nervous tissue
    • Isspecialisedfor internal communication by means ofelectrical and chemical signals.

    • Also, required process such as digestion, elimination ofwaste, breathing, blood circulation and production ofheat.

Q5. Define diffusion,osmolarityand tonicity.

Q5. Define diffusion,osmolarityand tonicity.

Diffusion is the net movement from a region of highconcentration to a region of low concentration. It is apassive or spontaneous process.

Osmolarityis the total concentration of solutes,including penetrating and non-penetrating solutes.

Tonicity reflects cells behaviour and depends on theconcentration of only the non-penetrating solutes. Isalways in reference of the extracellular non-penetratingsolute concentration (isotonic, hypertonic, hypotonic)

Q6. What factors affect the rate of diffusion through acell membrane?

Q6. What factors affect the rate of diffusion through a cellmembrane?

  • Lipid solubility
  • Molecular size
  • Cell membrane thickness
  • Concentration gradient
  • Membrane surface area
  • Composition of lipidlayer
  • NOT covered in lectures: Flick’sLaw of Diffusion
    • Rate of diffusion is proportional to surface area xconcentration gradient x membrane permeability /membrane thickness
    • Membrane permeability is proportional to lipid solubility /molecular size

Q7.What happens to the cell volume in an

a. Isotonic environment?

b. Hypotonic environment?

c. Hypertonic environment?

  • Isotonic has the same concentration of non-penetrating solutes, so cell volume remains constant
  • Hypotonic has a lower concentration of non-penetrating solutes, so cell volume increases
  • Hypertonic has a higher concentration of non-penetrating solutes, so cell volume decreases as it loseswater by osmosis
