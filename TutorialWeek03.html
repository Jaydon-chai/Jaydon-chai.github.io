Q2. What type of forces are trabecular bone and corticalbone good at resisting?

Q2. What type of forces are trabecular bone and corticalbone good at resisting?

  • Trabecular bone (or cancellous bone)
    • Is the spongy or less dense bone found at the ends oflong bones or near joints
    • Is good at resisting static forces (or weight bearingforces)
  • Cortical bone (or compact bone)
    • Is the dense bone on the outer surface of most bones
    • Is good at resisting dynamic (or bending) forces

Q3. Why are bursa important in the shoulder joint?

Q3. Why are bursa important in the shoulder joint?

  • Bursa are synovial sacks filled with synovial fluid
  • Bursa help to decrease friction between two movingstructures such as tendon and bone
  • Important in theglenohumeraljoint because of thecomplexity of muscular stability and the high degreeof mobility

Q4. Describe the extracellular matrix of bones. How does theextracellular matrix of bones relate to the strength and resilience ofbones?

  • 2/3 inorganic
    • 85% hydroxyapatite (crystallized calcium phosphate)
    • 10% calcium carbonate
    • Other minerals
  • 1/3 organic
    • Collagen fibres
    • Protein
    • Carbohydrates
  • The combination of minerals and collagen provides the strengthand resilience
    • Minerals resist compression
    • Collagen resists tension
    • Many other additional factors that can be discussed

    • Much looser in the shoulder joint
  • Muscles in the shoulder joint
    • Any muscles that cross theglenohumeraljoint can produce acompressive force between the head ofhumerusand theglenoid cavity to provide stability when required
  • Functional
    • Shoulder joint has a greater range of motion and not involvedin weight bearing
    • Hip joint has to be stable for the weight bearing, but alsoneeds some mobility
