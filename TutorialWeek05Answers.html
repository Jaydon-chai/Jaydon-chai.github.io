Q1.List the components of thick and thin filaments inskeletal muscles.

  • Thick filaments
    • Comprises of 100s of myosin proteins
    • Elastic filament
  • Thin filaments
    • Comprises of ~2 actin proteins (a string of globular actinsubunits)
    • Tropomyosin
    • Troponin

Q2.List 3 mechanisms by which the strength of askeletal muscle contraction can be altered.

  • Number of motor units recruited
  • Frequency of stimulation or frequency of actionpotentials activating the muscle
  • Resting length of muscle before stimulation

Q3.List 3 types of glial cells found in the central nervoussystem. Describe the function of each.

Q3.List 3 types of glial cells found in the central nervoussystem. Describe the function of each.

  • Neuroglia of CNS
    • Oligodendrocytes
      • Form myelin in the brain and spinal cord
    • Ependymal cells
      • Secrete and circulate cerebrospinal fluid
    • Microglia
      • Phagocytize and destroy dead and foreign material
    • Astrocytes
      • Form a supportive network, nourish and stimulate theneurons

Q7.What would happen to the membrane potential ofNeuron C when there are simultaneous action potentialsin Neuron A and Neuron B?

Neuron A will depolarise Neuron C.

Neuron B will hyperpolarise Neuron C.

The response of Neuron C will depend on the spatial andtemporal summation of depolarisation (excitatory) andhyperpolarisation (inhibition).
