Q1. What are four factors that influence blood flow inblood vessels? Do they increase or decrease blood flow?

Q1. What are four factors that influence blood flow inblood vessels? Do they increase or decrease blood flow?

  • Width of blood vessels
    • Reduction in width, increases resistance (proportionalto r4, decreases blood flow
  • Length of blood vessels
    • Increase in length, increases resistance, decreases bloodflow
  • Blood viscosity
    • Increase in viscosity, increases resistance, decreasesblood flow
  • Difference in pressure
    • The greater the pressure difference, increases blood flow

Q3. Define the following terms:

  • Systolic blood pressure
    • Maximum pressure exerted in the arteries
  • Diastolic blood pressure
    • Minimum pressure within arteries
  • Pulse pressure
    • A measure of strength of pressure wave
    • Pulse pressure = systolic BP–diastolic BP
  • Mean arterial blood pressure
    • Average pressure responsible for driving blood forward
    • MAP = diastolic BP + 1/3 Pulse pressure

Q4. What factors determine mean artery pressure(MAP)?

Q4. What factors determine mean artery pressure (MAP)?

  • MAP = Cardiac output (CO) x total peripheral resistance(TPR)
  • TPR is determined by the resistance of the arteries andveins
    • Influenced by stretch receptors, sympathetic activity,hormones
  • CO = stroke volume (SV) x heart rate (HR)
    • SV affected by
      • Length of muscle fibre (amount of venous return, which isinfluenced by skeletal muscle pump, respiratory pump and neuralcontrol of the venous system)
      • Contractility of the heart (sympathetic activity)
    • HR affected by
      • Intrinsic rate of depolarization (pacemaker cells)
      • Autonomic nervous system: sympathetic activity (increases HR) andparasympathetic activity (decreases HR)
